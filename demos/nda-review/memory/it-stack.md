# IT stack — Microsoft / Azure only (hard rule)

**Helios Consulting runs entirely on the Microsoft stack.** Our infrastructure, our data,
and our client deliverables live on **Microsoft Azure** and **Microsoft 365**. This is a
deliberate sovereignty, security, and compliance decision — not a preference.

## The rule

- We **cannot** sign an agreement that requires our confidential or client data to be
  **hosted, processed, or routed on Google Cloud Platform (GCP) or Google Workspace.**
- An NDA or contract that puts shared data on a Google environment is, for us,
  **impossible to sign and actively deleterious**: it would break our data-residency
  commitments to existing clients and our internal security baseline.
- This extends to sub-processors and collaboration tooling: if the counterparty's
  data-handling, hosting, or shared-workspace clause names **Google** (GCP, Google Drive,
  Google Workspace, Gmail for shared confidential material), it is a **deal-breaker** that
  must be flagged at the highest severity and escalated to Legal — not quietly accepted.

## Why this is in memory and not just "known"

The agent has no way to know our infrastructure choices from the contract text alone. A
generic, very capable contract reviewer would read a Google-hosting clause as ordinary and
wave it through. It is **only** this remembered fact that turns an innocuous-looking clause
into a deal-breaker. That is the whole point of the demo: experience (memory) changes the
judgment, exactly like a new hire who has learned how the company actually operates.

## Acceptable / preferred

- Microsoft Azure (any region we already use), Microsoft 365, Teams, SharePoint, OneDrive.
- Counterparties who are Google-based internally are fine **as long as the shared
  confidential data under this agreement stays on Microsoft/our environment.** The trigger
  is *our* data on Google, not the counterparty's own internal tooling.
